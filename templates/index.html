Comprehensive Guide to Indian Income Tax (FY 2025-26)

Filing income tax in India can be confusing, especially with the government offering two distinct tax structures: the New Tax Regime (Default) and the Old Tax Regime. The TaxMojo calculator is designed to help salaried employees, freelancers, and business owners instantly determine their exact tax liability and choose the structure that saves them the most money.

1. Income Tax Slabs for FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)

The Union Budget has made the New Tax Regime the default choice, offering wider slabs and lower rates, but eliminating over 70 standard exemptions. Below is the comparative slab structure:

Income Range New Regime Tax Rate Old Regime Tax Rate (< 60 Yrs)

₹3,00,001 - ₹6,00,000 5% 5% (₹2.5L to ₹5L)

2. Section 87A Tax Rebate Explained

The government provides a tax rebate under Section 87A, which essentially makes your income "tax-free" up to a certain limit by forgiving the calculated tax.

  • New Regime: Full rebate is available if your total taxable income is up to ₹7,00,000 (Effectively making income up to ₹7.75 Lakhs tax-free for salaried individuals due to the ₹75k standard deduction). Furthermore, "Marginal Relief" is now provided if your income slightly exceeds ₹7 Lakhs, ensuring you don't pay more tax than the income you earned above the threshold.
  • Old Regime: Full rebate is capped at a total taxable income of ₹5,00,000 (Max rebate amount of ₹12,500).

3. Deductions Allowed vs. Disallowed

The biggest decision taxpayers face is whether to give up their deductions for a lower flat rate. Here is what happens under the New Regime:

❌ Disallowed in New Regime

  • Section 80C (EPF, LIC, PPF, ELSS up to 1.5L)
  • Section 80D (Health Insurance Premiums)
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA - Section 10(13A))
  • Leave Travel Allowance (LTA)
  • Home Loan Interest (Section 24b)
  • Professional Tax (Section 16(iii))

✅ Allowed in New Regime

  • Standard Deduction on Salary (₹75,000)
  • Standard Deduction on Family Pension (₹25,000)
  • Employer Contribution to NPS (Section 80CCD(2))
  • Agniveer Corpus Fund Contributions (Section 80CCH(2))

4. How are Capital Gains & Crypto Taxed?

Profits made from selling assets are taxed at "Special Rates" and sit outside the standard slab brackets.

  • Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG): Equity stocks held for less than a year are taxed at a flat 20% (Sec 111A).
  • Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG): Equity held for over a year is taxed at 12.5%, but the first ₹1.25 Lakhs of profit is completely tax-free every year (Sec 112A).
  • Crypto / Virtual Digital Assets (VDA): Under Section 115BBH, any profit from crypto is taxed at a harsh flat rate of 30%. No deductions are allowed, the basic exemption limit does not apply, and you cannot offset crypto losses against any other profits.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Which tax regime is better for a salary of ₹10 Lakhs?

If you claim zero investments, the New Regime is significantly better. However, if you claim the full ₹1.5L under 80C, ₹50k in NPS, and have HRA or home loan interest, the Old Regime will likely yield a lower tax payout. Use the TaxMojo calculator above to see your exact break-even point.

Can I switch between the New and Old Regime every year?

If you are a salaried individual with no business or professional income, yes, you can choose between the regimes every single year when filing your ITR. However, if you have business income, you only get a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to switch back to the Old Regime.

What is the Health and Education Cess?

Once your base income tax and any applicable surcharge are calculated, the government adds an additional mandatory 4% "Cess" on top of that final tax amount to fund public healthcare and education initiatives. This applies to both the Old and New regimes.
